About Me

Hi! My name is Sheldon Chean and I'm an aspiring web developer based in Auckland, New Zealand.

I am currently a student at the University of Auckland, taking a Bachelor of Science in Computer Science and Information Systems. I decided to pursue this over Software Engineering as I believe the business skills and different teachings provides me a unique view on software development, mainly in producing software for clients.

The everchanging world of web development was intimidating to step into at first, but discovering the new frameworks and keeping up with the bleeding edge has been exciting. Despite coming from a more fundamental education in software development, I am always willing to learn the new ideas and techniques that lie in web development.

What are my interests outside of programming?

In my free time I am an avid competitor in Super Smash Bros Melee. Since joining the New Zealand scene I have made deep connections with people from all walks of life, experienced what goes into hosting large events, and greatly improved my problem solving skills. I also enjoy consuming all sorts of media, specifically literature and animation. Outside of these, I have a keen interest in fashion, graphic design, writing and learning Japanese.

gamestack

gamestack


gamestack is a website where users can register accounts and list the video games they own in their collection. Users are able to add and remove games from their 'stack', and also view the 'stacks' of other users. It features safe and persistent sessions, responsive design, and descriptive error handling.

This was my first approach at Node.js, Express.js and Mongoose. I learned a lot of important lessons when developing this, especially interacting with external APIs and the downsides of not using a front-end framework.

Check out the code and extended documentation here
MyTodoList

MyTodoList


MyTodoList is a minimal Single-page web app built on the MEAN stack, which allows users to log-in and register to interact with their personal to-do list.

I started this project as an introduction to Angular and how to integrate it with the rest of the MEAN stack, which I had been learning previously. It also features Passport authentication using JSON Web Tokens.

Check out the code and extended documentation here
